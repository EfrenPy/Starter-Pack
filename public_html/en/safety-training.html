Safety Training at CERN

Last updated: February 2026

All newcomers must complete mandatory safety training before being granted access to CERN sites. Plan to start the online modules as early as possible.

Mandatory Safety Course

Every person working at CERN must complete a general safety awareness course before accessing the sites. This course covers the essential topics you need to know to stay safe on CERN premises.

  • Fire safety and evacuation procedures
  • Electrical hazard awareness
  • Radiation awareness fundamentals
  • Emergency contacts and reporting

The course has both an online component (e-learning) and an on-site component. You can begin the online portion before arriving at CERN, which is strongly recommended to speed up the registration process.

Types of Safety Training

Depending on your role and the areas you will work in, you may need one or more of the following training courses:

  • General Safety (Level 1) — Mandatory for all personnel. Covers basic site safety, fire prevention, and evacuation.
  • Radiation Protection (Level 2/3) — Required for work in accelerator tunnels or experimental areas. Level depends on exposure category.
  • Electrical Safety — Required for anyone working on or near electrical installations.
  • Cryogenics Safety — Needed if you work near cryogenic systems, especially around the LHC.
  • Chemical Safety — For personnel handling chemical substances in laboratories.
  • Working at Heights — Required for tasks involving ladders, scaffolding, or elevated platforms.

E-Learning Platform

CERN provides safety e-learning modules through the Learning Hub platform. These modules can be completed before your arrival, allowing you to begin work more quickly once on site.

  • Access the Safety e-learning section on the CERN Learning Hub
  • Mandatory modules are flagged clearly in your training profile
  • Certificates are stored automatically in your profile upon completion
  • You can track your progress and see upcoming deadlines

On-Site Training

Some safety courses include practical on-site sessions that cannot be replaced by e-learning. These sessions provide hands-on experience with safety equipment and procedures.

  • Fire extinguisher handling practice
  • Evacuation drills for your building
  • Building-specific safety briefings from your safety officer
  • Schedule practical sessions through your supervisor or the HSE unit

Certification Renewal

Safety certifications are not permanent. Most need to be renewed periodically to ensure your knowledge stays up to date.

  • Most general certifications are valid for 3 to 5 years
  • Radiation protection training typically requires renewal every 3 years
  • The system sends you an automatic notification before your certification expires
  • Re-certification usually involves retaking the e-learning module and, in some cases, the on-site session

Specific Hazards at CERN

CERN is a unique environment with hazards not found in typical workplaces. Be aware of the following:

  • Radiation zones — CERN has supervised and controlled radiation areas. Access requires specific training and dosimetry equipment.
  • Cryogenic risks — The LHC operates at extremely low temperatures. Cryogenic leaks can displace oxygen and cause cold burns.
  • Electrical installations — High-voltage equipment is present in many areas. Only qualified personnel may work on these systems.
  • Confined spaces — Some areas have restricted ventilation. Entry requires a permit and specific training.
  • Overhead cranes — Heavy lifting equipment is used in experimental halls. Stay clear of marked zones during operations.

Safety Rules on Site

  • Wear your CERN badge visibly at all times while on site
  • Follow all zone access restrictions — never enter areas you are not authorized for
  • Report any incident or unsafe situation immediately to your safety officer
  • Never enter the accelerator tunnel without explicit authorization
  • Know the contact details of your building safety officer and the CERN Fire Brigade (phone 74444)
