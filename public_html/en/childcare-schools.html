Childcare & Schools Near CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Important

Waiting lists for childcare and popular schools can be very long. Start researching and registering as early as possible, ideally before your arrival at CERN.

CERN Childcare: Le Jardin des Particules

  • Le Jardin des Particules is the CERN on-site creche, accepting children from 4 months to 6 years of age.
  • It is managed by the CERN Staff Association and located on the Meyrin site. Places are reserved for children of CERN employees and associated personnel.
  • There is typically a significant waitlist. Apply as soon as you receive your CERN contract, or even earlier if possible, through CERN HR.
  • Fees are income-dependent and generally competitive compared to local private nurseries in the Geneva area.

Local Nurseries (Creches)

  • Swiss creches: Available in Meyrin, Vernier, and surrounding communes. Managed by the commune or private operators. Registration is usually done through the commune's early childhood office (Service de la petite enfance).
  • French creches: Available in Saint-Genis-Pouilly, Ferney-Voltaire, and nearby towns. Managed by the commune or associations. Contact the local Relais Petite Enfance for availability.
  • Registration timelines: Apply early — waiting lists of 6 months or more are common on both sides of the border. Some communes require registration before the child is born.
  • Cost comparison: Swiss creches tend to be more expensive (CHF 100-150/day) but fees are often income-adjusted. French creches are generally cheaper (EUR 5-30/day depending on income and CAF subsidies).

Primary & Secondary Schools

  • Swiss public schools: Free and French-language. Children are assigned to the school in their commune of residence. The school year starts in late August. Registration is done through the commune.
  • French public schools: Free and French-language. Registration is at the local mairie. The school year starts in September. Children are assigned based on their catchment area (carte scolaire).
  • School year differences: Swiss schools (Canton of Geneva) start in late August, while French schools start in September. School holidays also differ between the two countries.
  • Integration classes: Both Swiss and French public schools offer integration support (classes d'accueil in Geneva, UPE2A in France) for children who do not yet speak French.

International Schools

  • International School of Geneva (Ecolint): The oldest international school in the world, with multiple campuses (La Grande Boissiere, Nations, La Chataigneraie). Offers IB curriculum in English and French. Tuition ranges from CHF 20,000 to CHF 35,000 per year.
  • Campus des Nations: A campus of Ecolint located near the UN in Geneva, convenient for families living on the Swiss side.
  • Lycee International de Ferney-Voltaire: A French public school with international sections (English, German, etc.). Free tuition with a small fee for the international section. Very popular with CERN families on the French side.
  • Geneva English School: A private English-language primary school in Geneva following the English National Curriculum.
  • CERN education grants: CERN provides education grants for staff members' children attending school. This can partially offset international school tuition. Check with CERN HR for eligibility and amounts.

Higher Education & After-School Activities

  • Nearby universities: The University of Geneva (UniGE) and EPFL/University of Lausanne are within easy reach for older students or accompanying partners.
  • After-school programs: Swiss communes offer garderies (after-school care) and parascolaire activities. In France, centres aeres and garderies periscolaires provide similar services.
  • Sports clubs: Numerous sports clubs for children are available through local communes, the CERN Staff Association, and private organizations in the area.

Tips for Families

  • Register early: For creches and popular international schools, apply as soon as possible — ideally 6-12 months in advance.
  • CERN education grants: If your children attend a fee-paying school, check with CERN HR about education grant eligibility. The grant can cover a significant portion of tuition.
  • Check catchment areas: If you plan to use public schools, verify the catchment area (secteur scolaire) before choosing your housing.
  • Bilingual advantage: The Geneva region offers a unique bilingual environment. Children attending local schools will naturally develop French skills alongside their home language.
