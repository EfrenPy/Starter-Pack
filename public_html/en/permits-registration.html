Permits & Registration at CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Disclaimer

This page is for general informational purposes only. It does not constitute legal advice. Always consult with the CERN Users Office or a qualified advisor for your specific situation.

Overview

As staff of an international organisation, CERN employees have a special permit status. You do not need a standard Swiss or French work permit to work at CERN.

Your legal status is managed through CERN and the host state agreements with Switzerland and France. The CERN Users Office is your main point of contact for all permit and registration matters.

Swiss Carte de Legitimation

  • What it is: The Carte de Legitimation is an identity document issued by the DFAE (Swiss Federal Department of Foreign Affairs) that certifies your status as a member of an international organisation.
  • Types: The type depends on your nationality and contract. Common types include B and C (with full customs privileges), D and E (limited customs privileges), P (scientific personnel), and S (Swiss nationals). Types H, F, and G have no special customs privileges.
  • How to obtain: Obtained through the CERN Users Office, which processes the application with the DFAE on your behalf. You must register within 14 days of your arrival.
  • Validity: The carte de legitimation validity is linked to the duration of your CERN contract. It must be renewed if your contract is extended.
  • Benefits: The carte de legitimation exempts you from standard Swiss work and residence permits, and provides certain tax and customs advantages.

French Titre de Sejour

  • Who needs it: If you live in France (e.g. in the Pays de Gex), you need a titre de sejour (residence permit) in addition to your CERN carte de legitimation.
  • Special category: International organisation employees have a special titre de sejour category, different from standard work permits.
  • Where to apply: Apply at the prefecture of Ain (Bourg-en-Bresse) or the sub-prefecture corresponding to your place of residence.
  • Documents needed: Passport, proof of address, CERN contract, carte de legitimation, passport photos, completed application form.
  • Renewal: The titre de sejour must be renewed before its expiry date. Start the renewal process well in advance (at least 2 months before).

The 14-Day Rule

  • Obligation: You must register with the CERN Users Office within 14 days of your arrival. This is a strict requirement.
  • What it triggers: Registration triggers the carte de legitimation application process and establishes your legal status in Switzerland.
  • Late registration consequences: Late registration can delay your carte de legitimation, affect your health insurance coverage, and cause administrative complications.

Family Members

  • Dependent cards: Your spouse/partner and children receive their own legitimation cards as dependents of the primary holder.
  • Spouse/partner registration: Your spouse or registered partner must be declared to the Users Office. Common-law partners may have different requirements depending on nationality.
  • Children: Minor children are included in your registration. Adult children may require additional paperwork.
  • Documents needed: Marriage certificate, children's birth certificates, sworn translations into English or French if documents are in another language, Hague apostille if applicable.

Changing Residence

  • Switzerland to France: If you move from Switzerland to France, you must notify the CERN Users Office, apply for a French titre de sejour, and update your address.
  • France to Switzerland: If you move from France to Switzerland, you must register with the relevant Swiss commune and notify the Users Office.
  • New permits needed: A change of country of residence requires new permits. The process can take several weeks, so plan ahead.
  • Timeline: Notify the Users Office as soon as possible, ideally before the move. Deadline requirements vary by country.

Practical Tips

  • Start your paperwork immediately upon arrival. Do not wait.
  • Keep copies of all documents (physical and digital). You will need copies on multiple occasions.
  • The CERN Users Office is your main point of contact for any permit and registration questions.
  • Have passport photos ready (Swiss format: 35x45 mm) for cards and permits.
  • If you have family members, gather all necessary documents (certificates, translations) before arriving.
