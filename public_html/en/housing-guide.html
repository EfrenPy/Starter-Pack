Housing & Daily Life Near CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Note

Prices and details on this page are approximate and may change. Always verify with official sources before making decisions.

Switzerland vs France: Overview

Factor Switzerland (Geneva) France (Pays de Gex)
Rent Very high (CHF 1,800-3,000+/month for 2-3 rooms) Moderate (EUR 800-1,500/month for similar size)
Healthcare CHIS primary; LAMal optional for CERN staff CHIS primary; CMU/PUMA optional for residents
Commute 5-20 min by bus/bike from Meyrin area 10-30 min by car; bus options available
Language French (Geneva is French-speaking) French
Income tax CERN salary exempt from Swiss income tax CERN salary exempt; must file French return
Cost of living Very high (groceries, dining, services) Significantly lower than Switzerland

Popular Areas

In Switzerland

  • Meyrin: Closest to CERN's main entrance. Short commute, local shops and markets, suburban feel. Popular with CERN employees for convenience.
  • Vernier: Affordable by Geneva standards. Good bus connections to CERN. More residential, with a multicultural community.
  • Geneva centre: Vibrant city life, excellent amenities and nightlife. Higher rents and longer commute to CERN (20-30 min by bus).
  • Carouge: Charming neighbourhood south of Geneva centre. Artistic atmosphere, good restaurants. Slightly longer commute but well connected by tram.

In France

  • Saint-Genis-Pouilly: Directly adjacent to CERN. Many CERN employees live here. Good local amenities, supermarkets, and schools. Short drive or bus ride to CERN.
  • Ferney-Voltaire: Charming town with good schools and restaurants. Close to the Swiss border and Geneva airport. Popular with international families.
  • Gex: Small historic town at the foot of the Jura mountains. Quieter and more rural, with a weekly market. Slightly longer commute.
  • Thoiry: Residential area with good value housing. Close to skiing and outdoor activities in the Jura. Growing community of CERN families.
  • Annemasse: Larger town on the French-Swiss border south of Geneva. Lower rents, connected to Geneva by Leman Express train. Longer commute to CERN.

Finding Housing

  • CERN Housing Service: CERN offers a Housing Service that maintains a list of available rentals and can assist newcomers. Check the CERN internal website or contact the Users Office for details. Temporary on-site accommodation (CERN hostel) may be available for your first weeks.
  • Websites: In Switzerland: homegate.ch, immoscout24.ch, comparis.ch. In France: seloger.com, leboncoin.fr, bien-ici.com. Cross-border: CERN Market (internal) and local Facebook groups.
  • Facebook groups: Search for groups like "CERN Housing", "Expats in Geneva", "Pays de Gex Expats". These often have listings and useful advice from other CERN employees.
  • Scam warnings: Be wary of listings that seem too good to be true, request money upfront before viewing, or use suspicious payment methods. Always visit the property in person before signing any contract.

Transport

  • TPG: Transports Publics Genevois operates buses and trams in the Geneva area. Lines 66 and 68 serve CERN directly. A monthly pass (Unireso) covers all public transport in the Geneva zone.
  • Unireso: The integrated fare system covering TPG buses/trams, SBB trains, and Mouettes boats in the Greater Geneva area. Monthly passes available for different zones.
  • Leman Express: Regional train connecting Geneva to surrounding French towns (Annemasse, Annecy corridor). Useful if you live south of the border.
  • CERN shuttles: CERN operates internal shuttle buses between its sites (Meyrin and Prevessin campuses). Free for CERN personnel.
  • Cycling: The area is generally flat and cycle-friendly. CERN has bike racks at most buildings. Geneva has a bike-sharing scheme (Donkey Republic/Velospot).
  • Car: Many people living in France commute by car. Parking is available at CERN but can be limited. Cross-border commuters typically need a G permit for Switzerland.

Health Insurance

  • CHIS: The CERN Health Insurance Scheme is your primary coverage. It covers medical expenses in Switzerland, France, and abroad. All CERN employees and their registered dependents are enrolled automatically.
  • LAMal: Swiss mandatory health insurance. CERN staff with a carte de legitimation are exempt and do not need LAMal. However, family members without CERN status working in Switzerland may need to enrol.
  • CMU/PUMA: French universal healthcare. CERN employees living in France are generally exempt from compulsory affiliation thanks to CHIS coverage. If your partner works in France outside CERN, they would join the French system.

Banking

  • Swiss accounts: Traditional banks (UBS, BCGE) often require Swiss residency and charge significant fees. Useful for receiving salary in CHF and paying Swiss rent. CERN may help with introductions to local banks.
  • French accounts: Banks like BNP Paribas, Societe Generale, or online banks (Boursorama, Fortuneo) are options for those living in France. Needed for French rent payments and direct debits.
  • Multi-currency options: Digital banks like Wise, Revolut, and N26 are popular with CERN employees for low-fee currency exchange between CHF and EUR and international transfers.

Groceries & Shopping

  • Swiss supermarkets: Migros and Coop are the main chains. Lidl and Aldi offer lower prices. Shopping in Switzerland is significantly more expensive than in France.
  • French supermarkets: Carrefour, Leclerc, Lidl, and Intermarche in the Pays de Gex area offer much lower prices than Swiss equivalents. Many Swiss residents cross the border to shop in France.
  • CERN cafeterias: CERN has several restaurants and cafeterias on site (Restaurant 1, Restaurant 2, etc.) offering affordable meals. Coffee corners and vending machines are available throughout the campus.
