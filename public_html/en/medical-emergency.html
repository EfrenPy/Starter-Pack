Medical Services & Emergencies at CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Important

In a life-threatening emergency at CERN, dial 74444 (internal) or +41 22 767 4444 (external). Outside CERN, dial 144 (Switzerland) or 15 (France) for an ambulance, or 112 for the European emergency number.

CERN Medical Service

  • The CERN Medical Service is located in Building 57 on the Meyrin site. It provides occupational health services, general medical consultations, vaccinations, and medical certificates.
  • Opening hours: Monday to Friday, 8:00-12:00 and 13:30-17:00. The Medical Service is closed on weekends and CERN closure days.
  • Appointments can be booked by phone or through the CERN Medical Service website. Walk-ins may be accepted depending on availability.
  • No out-of-hours emergency service: The CERN Medical Service does not provide emergency care outside of opening hours. For emergencies outside working hours, call the CERN Fire Brigade (74444) or go to a hospital.

CERN Infirmary

  • The CERN Infirmary provides first aid and treatment for minor injuries and ailments on the CERN site during working hours.
  • The infirmary can handle minor cuts, bruises, headaches, and similar non-urgent conditions. It is not equipped for serious injuries or emergencies.
  • If you have a minor health issue during the workday, visit the infirmary first. For anything more serious, contact the CERN Fire Brigade (74444) or go to a hospital directly.

Emergency at CERN

  • CERN emergency number: Dial 74444 from any internal CERN phone, or +41 22 767 4444 from a mobile phone. This connects you to the CERN Fire Brigade, which coordinates all emergency responses on site.
  • The CERN Fire Brigade is trained to respond to medical emergencies, fires, chemical spills, and other incidents. They are available 24/7.
  • Defibrillators: Automated external defibrillators (AEDs) are available at various locations across the CERN site. Look for the green AED signs in buildings.
  • What to do in an emergency: Call 74444, state your location clearly (building and room number), describe the situation, do not hang up until told to do so, and send someone to meet the emergency team at the building entrance if possible.

Hospitals

  • HUG (Hopitaux Universitaires de Geneve): The main hospital in Geneva with a 24/7 emergency department (urgences). Located at Rue Gabrielle-Perret-Gentil 4, 1205 Geneva. Accessible by tram 15 or bus.
  • Centre Hospitalier du Pays de Gex: A smaller hospital located in Gex, France, closer to CERN for those living on the French side. It has an emergency department for less critical cases.
  • Meyrin medical centres: Several medical centres and clinics in Meyrin and surrounding areas offer walk-in appointments and minor urgent care during business hours.

Finding a Doctor

  • To find a general practitioner (medecin generaliste), you can register with a local doctor near your residence. In Switzerland, you do not always need a referral to see a specialist, but some insurance plans require one.
  • Swiss system: Use online directories or ask your commune for a list of local doctors accepting new patients. Waiting lists can be long in the Geneva area.
  • French system: Register with a medecin traitant (primary care doctor) through the French healthcare system. This is recommended to receive full reimbursement rates.
  • The CERN Medical Service can recommend doctors and specialists in the area. Ask at your next appointment or contact them by phone.

Pharmacies

  • Swiss pharmacies: Several pharmacies are located in Meyrin and Vernier, close to CERN. They are open during standard business hours and can dispense prescriptions from Swiss or CERN doctors.
  • French pharmacies: Pharmacies in Saint-Genis-Pouilly and Ferney-Voltaire serve the French side of the border. They accept French prescriptions and some Swiss prescriptions.
  • Sunday/night pharmacies (pharmacie de garde): A rotating schedule determines which pharmacy is open on Sundays, holidays, and at night. Check the local pharmacie de garde schedule online or on the pharmacy door.
  • Prescriptions from CERN Medical Service are generally accepted at both Swiss and French pharmacies. Keep your CHIS card with you for reimbursement purposes.

Mental Health

  • CERN provides psychological support through the CERN Medical Service. You can request an appointment with a psychologist through the standard booking process.
  • Employee Assistance Programme (EAP): CERN offers a confidential Employee Assistance Programme that provides short-term counselling, crisis support, and referral services. This service is available to all CERN personnel and their family members.
  • External resources include private psychologists and psychiatrists in the Geneva area. The CERN Medical Service can provide referrals.
  • Confidentiality: All mental health consultations, whether through the CERN Medical Service or the EAP, are strictly confidential and are not shared with your supervisor or HR department.
