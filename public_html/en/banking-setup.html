Banking Setup Near CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Note

Banking products, fees, and requirements may change. Always verify with the bank directly before opening an account.

Why You Need Bank Accounts

  • CERN pays salaries in Swiss francs (CHF), so a Swiss bank account is essential for receiving your salary.
  • If you live in France, you will need a French bank account for rent payments, utilities, and direct debits in euros (EUR).
  • Living near the Swiss-French border means you deal with two currencies daily. Having accounts in both currencies simplifies payments and avoids costly exchange fees.

Swiss Bank Accounts

  • Main banks: UBS and BCGE (Banque Cantonale de Geneve) are the most commonly used by CERN employees. Credit Suisse has been integrated into UBS.
  • Required documents: Carte de legitimation (issued by CERN/DFAE), valid passport or ID, employment contract or CERN attestation, and proof of address (if available).
  • Typical fees: CHF 5-15 per month for account maintenance, depending on the bank and account type. Some banks offer reduced fees for CERN staff.
  • Opening process: Book an appointment at the branch (in-person required for most Swiss banks). Bring all documents. The account is usually active within a few business days. A debit card (Maestro or Visa Debit) is issued by post.

French Bank Accounts

  • Traditional banks: BNP Paribas, Societe Generale, and Credit Agricole have branches in Saint-Genis-Pouilly and Ferney-Voltaire near CERN.
  • Online banks: Boursorama and Fortuneo offer fee-free current accounts and are popular with CERN employees living in France. Fully digital application process.
  • Required documents: Titre de sejour or attestation from the prefecture, valid passport or ID, proof of address in France (utility bill or rental contract), and employment contract.
  • Opening process: Traditional banks require an in-person appointment. Online banks can be opened remotely in a few days. A French RIB (Releve d'Identite Bancaire) is needed for rent and utility payments.

On-Site Banking at CERN

  • UBS branch: There is a UBS branch located at CERN (Building 504) specifically serving CERN personnel. Staff there are experienced with the carte de legitimation and CERN employment contracts.
  • ATMs: UBS and BCGE ATMs are available on the CERN Meyrin site for cash withdrawals in CHF.
  • Opening hours: The on-site UBS branch typically operates on limited hours (mornings only on certain weekdays). Check the CERN website or internal directory for current hours.
  • Appointments: It is recommended to book an appointment in advance, especially during arrival periods (September-October) when many newcomers open accounts simultaneously.

Multi-Currency & Digital Banks

  • Wise (formerly TransferWise): Very popular among CERN employees. Offers multi-currency accounts with real exchange rates and low fees. Ideal for CHF/EUR conversions and international transfers.
  • Revolut: Provides multi-currency accounts, free card payments abroad, and competitive exchange rates. Useful for daily cross-border spending.
  • N26: German digital bank offering free euro accounts. Available to French residents. Good as a secondary EUR account.
  • Limitations: Digital banks may not always be accepted for salary deposits by CERN. They also may lack French RIB functionality needed for some direct debits. Best used as complementary accounts rather than primary ones.

Salary Payment

  • Currency: CERN pays all salaries in Swiss francs (CHF). You will need to provide a CHF-denominated IBAN to the HR department.
  • Split payments: CERN allows you to split your salary between multiple bank accounts (e.g., part in CHF to a Swiss account, part converted to EUR to a French account). Contact HR/payroll to set this up.
  • First payment: Your first salary payment may take 4-6 weeks after your start date. Plan to have enough savings to cover your initial expenses (deposit, rent, living costs) during this period.

Tips

  • Open Swiss first: Prioritise opening a Swiss bank account as soon as possible after arrival so you can receive your salary without delay.
  • French account if needed: If you live in France, open a French account for rent, utilities, and local payments. Many landlords require a French RIB for direct debit.
  • Use Wise/Revolut daily: For everyday cross-border spending (groceries in France, coffee in Switzerland), digital banks offer the best exchange rates and lowest fees.
  • Avoid airport exchanges: Currency exchange offices at Geneva airport and in tourist areas charge high commissions. Use your bank card or digital bank instead.
