Clubs & Social Life at CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Important

Some popular sports clubs and courses have limited spots and fill up quickly at the start of each season. Register early and check the Staff Association website regularly for openings.

Staff Association

  • The CERN Staff Association is the elected body representing all CERN personnel. It advocates for employees' rights, working conditions, and benefits.
  • Beyond representation, the Staff Association organises and manages a wide range of clubs, activities, and social events throughout the year.
  • Membership in the Staff Association gives you access to subsidised club memberships, group insurance rates, discounts at local businesses, and various perks.
  • The Staff Association offices are located in Building 504 on the Meyrin site. Visit their website or drop by for information on all available activities.

Welcome Club

  • For newcomers: The Welcome Club is specifically designed for newcomers to CERN and their families. It is one of the best ways to start building your social network.
  • Social events: Regular coffee mornings, cultural outings, day trips to Swiss and French destinations, and seasonal celebrations bring members together.
  • Language courses: The Welcome Club offers affordable French classes and conversation groups, particularly useful for partners who may not work at CERN.
  • Playgroups: For families with young children, the Welcome Club organises playgroups where children and parents can socialise together.
  • How to join: Visit the Welcome Club website or attend one of their regular welcome events. Membership is open to all CERN personnel and their families.

Sports Clubs

  • Over 70 clubs: CERN has more than 70 sports clubs and courses covering a huge range of activities — from football, running, and swimming to skiing, tennis, yoga, martial arts, and cycling.
  • Subsidised gym: The CERN fitness centre offers subsidised access for all personnel. Equipped with modern machines, free weights, and group class studios.
  • Competitive teams: Several clubs field competitive teams that participate in local and regional leagues — football, basketball, volleyball, and more.
  • Registration: Browse the full list of sports clubs on the Staff Association website. Most clubs have an annual membership fee (typically modest) and welcome all skill levels.

Cultural Clubs

  • Music: The CERN music club, choir, and various bands bring together musicians of all levels. Concerts and jam sessions are held throughout the year.
  • Photography & art: Photography and art clubs organise exhibitions, workshops, and outings. A great way to explore the region creatively.
  • Wine & cooking: Wine-tasting clubs and cooking groups let you explore Swiss, French, and international cuisine with fellow enthusiasts.
  • Film, theatre & dance: Film clubs, theatre groups, and dance classes (salsa, tango, swing, and more) provide cultural entertainment and creative outlets.
  • Board games & more: Board game nights, book clubs, and other hobby groups meet regularly. Check the Staff Association directory for the full list.

National Communities

  • Country-specific groups: Many nationalities have their own informal communities at CERN — Spanish, Italian, Greek, German, Portuguese, Indian, and many others.
  • Cultural events: National groups organise celebrations for national holidays, traditional food events, and cultural presentations throughout the year.
  • Mutual support: These communities provide practical advice for compatriots — from finding a flat to navigating local bureaucracy in your native language.
  • Finding your community: Ask colleagues from your country, check the CERN e-groups mailing lists, or look for your national community on social media and messaging platforms.

Outdoor Activities

  • Hiking: The Jura mountains and the Alps are at your doorstep. Hundreds of marked trails range from easy lakeside walks to challenging alpine routes.
  • Skiing: The closest ski areas include Col de la Faucille and Crozet in the Jura (30 min drive), with major Alpine resorts like Chamonix, Verbier, and Flaine within 1-2 hours.
  • Lake Geneva: Swimming, sailing, paddle boarding, and lakeside relaxation. Public beaches (plages) are free and well-maintained around the lake.
  • Cycling: Excellent cycling infrastructure in both Switzerland and France. The CERN cycling club organises regular group rides and can recommend routes for all levels.
  • Staff Association excursions: The Staff Association organises group excursions — ski weekends, hiking trips, and cultural outings at subsidised prices.

Social Events

  • Hardronic Festival: CERN's annual music festival organised by the Staff Association, featuring live bands, food stalls, and a lively atmosphere on the CERN site.
  • Summer BBQs: Departmental and group barbecues are a CERN tradition during the summer months. A relaxed way to socialise with colleagues outside the office.
  • Christmas market: The annual CERN Christmas market brings seasonal treats, handmade crafts, and festive cheer to the Meyrin site each December.
  • Public events: CERN regularly hosts public lectures, open days, and science exhibitions. These are excellent opportunities to invite family and friends to discover CERN.
  • Science cafes: Informal science talks held at local cafes and venues around Geneva, organised by CERN outreach. Open to everyone and a great way to meet people beyond the CERN bubble.

Tips

  • Check the Staff Association website: The most up-to-date list of all clubs, activities, and events is maintained on the Staff Association website. Bookmark it and check regularly.
  • Attend newcomer events: Welcome sessions and newcomer events are organised regularly. These are the easiest way to meet people and learn about available activities.
  • Join early: Popular sports clubs and courses fill up fast, especially at the start of the academic year (September). Register as soon as enrollment opens.
  • Build your network: Joining clubs and attending social events is one of the best ways to build a strong personal and professional network at CERN. Do not hesitate to try something new.
