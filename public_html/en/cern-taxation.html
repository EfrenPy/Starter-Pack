CERN Taxation System

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Disclaimer

This page is for general informational purposes only. It does not constitute tax or legal advice. Always consult a qualified tax advisor for your specific situation.

CERN Internal Tax

  • CERN levies its own internal tax on salaries. This tax is deducted directly from your pay slip and is not a national tax.
  • The rates are progressive, similar in structure to a national income tax, but administered internally by CERN.
  • The internal tax has been in force since 1 January 2005, pursuant to the Protocol on Privileges and Immunities signed by Member States in 2004 and the CERN Council decision of 15 September 2005.
  • The internal tax is mandatory for all CERN staff and is calculated automatically each month.

National Income Tax Exemption

  • CERN salaries are exempt from national income tax under the Protocol on Privileges and Immunities of international organisations.
  • This exemption applies in the host states (Switzerland and France) and in most CERN member states.
  • The exemption applies only to CERN salary. Other income (investments, rental, freelance work) remains subject to normal taxation.

French Citizens Exception

  • French nationals working at CERN and living in France may be subject to French income tax on their CERN salary.
  • Special provisions apply and the situation can be complex. If you are a French citizen, you must consult a tax advisor.
  • This exception applies regardless of the type of contract you hold at CERN.

Swiss Residents

  • No Swiss income tax applies to CERN salary. However, wealth tax (impot sur la fortune) still applies at cantonal and communal level.
  • Non-CERN income (rental, investments, self-employment) is subject to Swiss cantonal and communal taxes.
  • You must file an annual Swiss tax return, declaring your wealth and any non-CERN income.

French Residents

  • For non-French nationals, no French income tax applies to CERN salary. However, you must still file an annual French tax return.
  • You must declare your worldwide income, including your CERN salary, but the CERN salary is declared as exempt income.
  • Non-CERN income (rental, investments) is subject to French income tax at standard rates.
  • The taux effectif may apply: the exempt CERN salary can be taken into account when calculating the tax rate applicable to your other income.

Outside Income

  • Income from investments, rental property, freelance work, or other sources IS taxable in your country of residence.
  • You must report all non-CERN income in your annual tax return in your country of residence.
  • Double taxation treaties between countries may affect how this income is taxed. Consult a tax advisor.

Spain-Specific Considerations

CERN employees who maintain their tax residence in Spain have specific obligations. The CERN salary is exempt from IRPF, but it must be declared.

Additionally, Modelo 720 obligations for declaring foreign assets apply when the established thresholds are exceeded.

Practical Tips

  • Keep all CERN pay slips. You will need them for your tax returns.
  • Always declare your CERN salary in your tax return, even if it is exempt. Not declaring it can cause problems.
  • Consult a qualified tax advisor if you have complex situations (dual residence, multiple income sources, etc.).
  • Tax treaties between countries matter. Your situation may vary depending on your nationality and country of residence.
