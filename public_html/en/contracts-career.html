Contracts & Career at CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Disclaimer

This page is for general informational purposes only. Always consult CERN HR or the Staff Association for up-to-date information on your contractual situation.

Contract Types

  • Staff: Indefinite or limited duration contract. The most stable contract type at CERN, with full access to all benefits.
  • Fellow: Limited duration contract, maximum 3 years. Aimed at post-doctoral or early-career professionals.
  • Doctoral Student: Programme of maximum 3 years for doctoral students registered at a home university.
  • Associate: Staff sent by member state institutes to work on specific CERN projects.
  • User: Affiliated researcher who uses CERN facilities but is not a direct employee.

Staff Contracts

  • Limited duration (LD): Staff contracts typically start as limited duration (LD), which can be extended up to a maximum of 8 years in total. During this period you may apply for an indefinite contract.
  • Indefinite duration: After the LD contract review, an indefinite duration contract may be granted. This depends on performance and CERN's needs.
  • Grades and steps: CERN staff are classified into grades (reflecting level of responsibility) and steps (reflecting experience). Step advancement is annual.
  • Career paths: Technical, administrative, and research career paths exist, each with its own progression criteria.

Fellowship Programme

  • Profile: Aimed at post-doctoral or early-career professionals in physics, engineering, computing, or other relevant disciplines.
  • Duration: Maximum 3 years. Fellowship contracts cannot be extended beyond this limit.
  • Competitive selection: The selection process is highly competitive, with periodic calls and evaluation committees.
  • One-time opportunity: The fellowship programme is a one-time opportunity. You cannot re-apply for the same programme after completing it.
  • Remuneration: Fellows receive a monthly stipend plus installation, travel, and, where applicable, family allowances.

Doctoral Students

  • Programme: The CERN Doctoral Student programme allows doctoral research to be carried out at CERN facilities.
  • Duration: Maximum 3 years. Students must be enrolled at a home university throughout the entire period.
  • Stipend: Doctoral students receive a monthly stipend and allowances. Conditions differ from those of fellows.

Extension Rules

  • Staff LD: Limited duration staff contracts are reviewed at 5 years. Extension or conversion to indefinite depends on performance and needs.
  • Fellows: Fellowship contracts cannot be extended beyond 3 years in total. There are no exceptions to this rule.
  • Maximum cumulative employment: Rules exist on the maximum cumulative duration of employment at CERN under different contract types.
  • Gaps between contracts: Mandatory gaps may be required between certain consecutive contract types.

Career Development

  • Annual reviews (MARS): All staff participate in the annual MARS (Merit Appraisal and Recognition of Staff) review system, which evaluates performance and sets objectives.
  • Training: CERN offers extensive technical, language, and professional development training opportunities through the Learning Hub.
  • Internal mobility: Mobility between departments and projects is encouraged to broaden experience and skills.
  • Promotion: Promotion criteria include performance, competencies, experience, and availability of positions at higher grades.
  • Career counselling: CERN offers career counselling services to help staff plan their career path.

Leaving CERN

  • Notice periods: Notice periods vary by contract type and seniority. Check your contract or with HR for exact timelines.
  • End-of-contract procedures: Include returning equipment, closing accounts, pension procedures, and updating permits.
  • Indemnities: Depending on contract type and length of service, you may be entitled to end-of-contract indemnities.
  • Pension options: Your pension options depend on your years of service. See the Pension Fund page for details.
  • Repatriation grant: Expatriate staff may be entitled to a repatriation grant at the end of their contract.
  • Certificate of employment: CERN issues a certificate of employment at the end of your contract, detailing your position and period of service.

Practical Tips

  • Understand your contract type and its duration limits from day one.
  • Plan ahead for the end of your contract, especially if you are a fellow or doctoral student.
  • Use CERN's career counselling services to plan your career path.
  • Keep track of your MARS reviews and professional achievements.
