Language Training at CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Important

CERN language courses are very popular and fill up quickly. Register as soon as enrollment opens to secure your spot, especially for French beginner levels.

Working Language at CERN

  • CERN's two official languages are English and French. All official documents and communications are available in both languages.
  • In practice, most day-to-day technical work, meetings, and emails are conducted in English. You can work comfortably at CERN without speaking French.
  • However, French is extremely useful for daily life outside CERN — shopping, dealing with local administration, visiting doctors, and socialising with neighbours.
  • There is no strict requirement to speak French to work at CERN, but learning at least basic French will significantly improve your quality of life in the Geneva region.

CERN Language Courses

  • Languages offered: CERN offers subsidised courses in French, English, German, Spanish, Italian, and occasionally other languages depending on demand.
  • Subsidised by CERN: Courses are heavily subsidised for CERN personnel. You only pay a small fraction of the actual cost, making them an excellent value.
  • Schedule: Classes are offered during and after working hours. Lunchtime and evening slots are the most popular. Each course typically runs for one semester.
  • Levels: Courses follow the CEFR framework (A1 to C2). A placement test determines your starting level. Most newcomers start at A1 or A2 for French.
  • Enrollment: Register through the CERN Learning Hub (learning.cern.ch). Enrollment periods are announced via email and on the CERN intranet. Act fast — courses fill up within hours.

Welcome Club Language Classes

  • Affordable group classes: The CERN Welcome Club offers affordable French classes aimed primarily at partners and family members of CERN personnel.
  • Focus on beginners: Classes focus mainly on French for beginners and intermediate learners, with an emphasis on practical everyday communication.
  • Social aspect: Beyond language learning, Welcome Club classes are a great way to meet other newcomers and build a social network in the CERN community.
  • Registration: Check the Welcome Club website for current offerings, schedules, and registration details. Classes typically start at the beginning of each semester.

CAGI Resources

  • CAGI: The Centre d'Accueil de la Geneve Internationale helps international workers and their families settle in Geneva, including language support.
  • Conversation exchange: CAGI organises conversation exchange programs where you can practice French with native speakers who want to practice your language in return.
  • Tandem partners: Sign up for tandem language partnerships — you meet regularly with a partner and split the time between practising each other's languages.
  • Integration workshops: CAGI runs workshops on Swiss and French culture, local customs, and practical life topics — all helpful for language and cultural integration.

Local Options

  • Alliance Francaise de Geneve: Well-established French language school offering group and private courses at all levels. Recognised certificates available.
  • Universite Populaire du Canton de Geneve: Offers affordable French courses for all levels, as well as other languages and cultural classes.
  • Commune integration courses (France): If you live in France (Pays de Gex area), your local commune or communaute de communes may offer free or low-cost French integration courses.
  • Migros Club School: Switzerland's largest adult education provider offers French and other language courses at competitive prices across Geneva canton.
  • Online platforms: Platforms like italki and Preply connect you with professional French tutors for private online lessons at flexible times and prices.

Self-Study & Apps

  • Duolingo: Free app with gamified French lessons. Good for building vocabulary and basic grammar, especially for absolute beginners.
  • Babbel: Subscription-based app with structured French courses designed by linguists. More in-depth grammar explanations than Duolingo.
  • TV5Monde: Free French-language media platform with news, shows, and dedicated language-learning exercises for all CEFR levels.
  • RFI — Journal en francais facile: Daily news broadcast in simplified French. Excellent for intermediate learners to improve listening comprehension.
  • Immersion: Listen to French radio (France Inter, RTS La Premiere), watch French TV (TF1, France 2), and switch your phone and apps to French for passive daily exposure.

Tips for Learning

  • Start before arriving: If possible, begin learning basic French before you move to Geneva. Even a few weeks of self-study will make your first days much easier.
  • Register early: CERN courses fill up within hours of opening. Set a reminder for the enrollment date and register immediately when it opens.
  • Practice with locals: Use every opportunity to speak French — at the market, in shops, at the boulangerie. Locals appreciate the effort even if your French is not perfect.
  • Join social activities: Attend French-speaking social events, conversation groups, or join a local sports club where French is the common language.
