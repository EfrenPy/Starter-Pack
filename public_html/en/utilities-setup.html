Utilities Setup Near CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Important

Utility systems differ significantly between Switzerland and France. Start setting up accounts before your move-in date to avoid delays.

Overview

  • Switzerland and France have different utility providers, billing systems, and regulations. Your setup process depends entirely on which side of the border you live.
  • In Switzerland, many utilities are bundled with the rental contract or handled automatically by the landlord. In France, tenants typically need to open their own accounts.
  • Plan to set up utilities 1-2 weeks before your move-in date. Internet installation may take longer, especially in France.

Electricity & Gas

  • Switzerland (Geneva): SIG (Services Industriels de Geneve) is the sole provider for electricity, gas, and water in the Canton of Geneva. Your account is typically activated automatically when you sign your rental contract. You may need to contact SIG to confirm the transfer.
  • France: EDF is the main electricity provider and Engie (formerly GDF Suez) for gas. You need to open an account by calling or registering online. Setup takes approximately 2 weeks. Have your new address and move-in date ready.
  • French monthly costs: Expect approximately EUR 80-150 per month for electricity and gas combined, depending on apartment size and heating type.
  • Meter readings: Take note of your electricity and gas meter readings on the day you move in. This protects you from being charged for the previous tenant's consumption.

Water

  • Switzerland: Water is provided by SIG and is usually included in the rental charges (charges communes). You typically do not need a separate water subscription.
  • France: Water supply varies by commune. In some areas it is included in the communal charges; in others, you may need a separate subscription with the local water provider (e.g., Suez, Veolia, or the communal water service).
  • Tap water quality: Tap water is safe to drink on both sides of the border. Geneva and the Pays de Gex area have excellent water quality.

Internet & Mobile

  • Switzerland: Main providers are Swisscom, Sunrise, and Salt. Fibre optic is widely available in the Geneva area. Expect to pay CHF 50-80 per month for broadband internet.
  • France: Main providers are Orange, Free (very popular, EUR 20-40/month), SFR, and Bouygues Telecom. Free's Freebox is particularly popular for its competitive pricing and includes a TV service.
  • Cross-border options: If you live in France and work at CERN, consider a French mobile plan with EU roaming (covers Switzerland for calls). Some providers offer specific cross-border packages.
  • CERN Wi-Fi: CERN provides eduroam Wi-Fi on all sites. Your CERN credentials give you access. You can also use eduroam at universities and research institutions worldwide.
  • eSIM: Most modern phones support eSIM. This can be convenient for having both a Swiss and French number without carrying two physical SIM cards.

TV License

  • Switzerland: The Serafe fee is CHF 335 per year and is mandatory for all households, regardless of whether you own a TV or radio. It is billed separately and cannot be avoided.
  • France: The contribution a l'audiovisuel public has been phased out as of 2022. You no longer need to pay a TV license fee in France.

Waste & Recycling

  • Switzerland: Geneva uses taxed garbage bags (sacs taxe) costing approximately CHF 2 per 35-litre bag. You must use official bags purchased from supermarkets. Recycling is strongly encouraged and free — separate paper, glass, PET, aluminium, and organic waste.
  • Dechetterie: Each Swiss commune has a recycling centre (dechetterie) where you can drop off bulky items, electronics, and hazardous waste for free.
  • France: Waste collection is included in local taxes (taxe d'habitation/taxe fonciere). Sorting rules are less strict than in Switzerland, but recycling is increasingly required. Yellow bins are for recyclables, green for glass.

Tips

  • Open accounts early: Contact utility providers 1-2 weeks before moving in to ensure everything is active on your arrival date.
  • Keep all contracts: Store copies of all utility contracts and correspondence. You will need them for administrative procedures and when moving out.
  • Bundled packages: In France, consider bundling internet, mobile, and TV with the same provider (e.g., Free) for savings. In Switzerland, Swisscom and Sunrise offer similar bundles.
  • Meter readings at move-in: Always note electricity, gas, and water meter readings when you move in and when you move out. Take photos as evidence.
