Transportation Near CERN

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Note

Schedules, fares, and routes may change. Always check official transport websites for the latest information.

Getting to CERN

  • Meyrin site: The main CERN campus is located in Meyrin, Switzerland, accessible via Entrance B on Route de Meyrin. This is where most offices, restaurants, and the main reception are located.
  • Prevessin site: The second CERN campus is in Prevessin-Moens, France. It hosts several experimental areas and offices. A free CERN shuttle connects the two sites.
  • Main options: You can reach CERN by public bus, CERN shuttle, bicycle, car, or regional train (to nearby stations). The best option depends on where you live.

Public Transport (TPG)

  • Bus line 68: The most important bus for CERN employees. It runs from Geneva city centre (Rive) through Meyrin to the CERN stop. Frequency is every 10-15 minutes during peak hours.
  • Tram 18: Tram line 18 runs from Plan-les-Ouates through Geneva to CERN (the terminus). It is the fastest public transport link between Geneva centre and CERN, taking about 25 minutes from Cornavin station.
  • Unireso zones and passes: The Geneva area uses the Unireso fare system. A monthly pass costs approximately CHF 70 and covers all TPG buses, trams, and local trains within the selected zones.
  • Buying tickets: Tickets can be purchased via the TPG app (recommended), at ticket machines at major stops, or on board (with a surcharge). Contactless payment is accepted on some machines.

CERN Shuttle

  • Free service: CERN operates a free internal shuttle bus between the Meyrin and Prevessin sites. Available to all CERN personnel and visitors with a valid access card.
  • Schedule: The shuttle runs every 15-20 minutes during standard working hours (approximately 08:00 to 18:00, Monday to Friday). Check the CERN transport page for the current timetable.
  • Route: The shuttle connects key buildings on both sites, with stops at the main entrances and major building clusters.

Cycling

  • Flat terrain: The area around CERN is generally flat, making cycling a practical and popular commuting option, especially from Meyrin and Saint-Genis-Pouilly.
  • Bike racks: CERN provides bike racks at most buildings across both sites. Covered racks are available near main entrances.
  • Bike-sharing: Geneva offers bike-sharing services such as Donkey Republic. Stations are available near CERN and throughout the city.
  • Bike paths: Dedicated bike paths connect Meyrin and Saint-Genis-Pouilly to CERN. The route from Geneva centre follows well-marked paths along Route de Meyrin.
  • Electric bikes: Electric bike rental services are available in Geneva and nearby towns. Some CERN employees use e-bikes for the slightly longer commute from Ferney-Voltaire or Gex.

Driving & Parking

  • Parking: Parking is available at both CERN sites but can be limited, especially at Meyrin during peak hours. A CERN access card is required for the parking barriers.
  • Cross-border commuting: Many employees living in France commute by car. The border crossing near CERN (Entrance E) is usually quick but can have delays during rush hour.
  • French motorway vignette: Not needed for local roads in the Pays de Gex area. Only required if using French motorways further afield.
  • Swiss vignette: A Swiss motorway vignette costs CHF 40 per year and is required for using Swiss motorways. Available at border crossings, post offices, and petrol stations.

Regional Trains

  • Leman Express: The regional train network connecting Geneva to Annemasse and the Annecy corridor. Useful if you live south of the border or in towns along the line.
  • SBB/CFF: Swiss Federal Railways operates frequent trains between Geneva (Cornavin) and Lausanne, as well as connections to the rest of Switzerland. The Geneva station is about 20 minutes from CERN by bus.
  • For longer commutes: If you live further from CERN (e.g., Nyon, Lausanne, Annemasse), combining train and bus can be an efficient commute option.

From Geneva Airport

  • By bus/tram: Take bus 23, 28, or 57 from the airport to Blandonnet, then transfer to tram 18 or bus 68 towards CERN. The total journey takes about 25-35 minutes depending on connections.
  • By taxi: A taxi from Geneva airport to CERN costs approximately CHF 40-50 and takes about 10-15 minutes by car depending on traffic.
  • Proximity: CERN is very close to Geneva airport (approximately 5 km). This makes it convenient for international travel and for visitors arriving by air.
