CERN Pension Fund

Last updated: February 2026

⚠️ Disclaimer

This page is for general informational purposes only. It does not constitute financial advice. Always consult a qualified financial advisor and CERN HR for your specific situation.

Overview

  • The CERN Pension Fund (CERN PF) is a defined-benefit scheme. This means your pension is calculated based on your years of service and salary, not just the contributions made.
  • Membership in the CERN PF is mandatory for all CERN staff and fellows.
  • The CERN PF is one of the largest international organisation pension funds, with professionally managed investments.

Contributions

  • Employee contribution: Approximately 10.1% of your monthly salary is automatically deducted as your pension fund contribution.
  • CERN contribution: CERN contributes approximately 20.2% of your salary, i.e. double your personal contribution.
  • Total: The total contribution is approximately 30.3% of your monthly salary, shared between you and CERN.
  • Automatic deduction: Contributions are deducted monthly from your pay slip. No action is required on your part.

Benefits by Years of Service

  • Less than 5 years: If you leave CERN with fewer than 5 years of service, you receive a lump-sum transfer value. You are not entitled to a pension.
  • 5 to 10 years: You can choose between a deferred pension (paid upon reaching retirement age) or a transfer value to another pension scheme.
  • 10+ years: You are entitled to a pension upon reaching retirement age. The amount depends on your years of service and average salary.
  • 15+ years: Full pension eligibility. Access to all retirement options, including early retirement with a smaller reduction.

Retirement Ages

  • Normal retirement: Age 65 if you joined the Pension Fund on or before 31 December 2011, or age 67 if you joined on or after 1 January 2012.
  • Early retirement: Possible from age 50 (pre-2012 members) or age 52 (post-2012 members) with at least 5 years of service, but with an actuarial reduction for each year of advancement.
  • Late retirement: It is possible to delay retirement beyond age 65 in certain circumstances, with an increase in the pension.
  • Flexible transition: Options exist for a gradual transition to retirement, including part-time work before full retirement.

Transfer Value

  • What it is: The transfer value is a sum calculated based on your accumulated contributions plus interest. It is offered to those leaving CERN before pension eligibility.
  • Transfer to another scheme: The value can be transferred to your new employer's pension scheme or to the national pension system of your country.
  • Process and timing: You must elect what to do with your transfer value within 3 months of leaving CERN. Payment or transfer is processed once your request is submitted.

Pension Calculation

  • Calculation basis: The pension is calculated based on your years of service and your average salary over the last years.
  • Accrual rate: 2% per year of service (pre-2012 members, max 35 years) or 1.85% per year (post-2012 members, max 37 years and 10 months).
  • Maximum pension: The pension may not exceed your final basic salary. The maximum accrual is 70% for pre-2012 members (35 × 2%) or approximately 70% for post-2012 members.
  • Survivor's pension: In case of death, your spouse and dependents may receive a survivor's pension.
  • Disability pension: If you become permanently incapacitated, you may be entitled to a disability pension.

Leaving CERN

  • Options: The options available depend on your years of service. Review all alternatives carefully before deciding.
  • Election deadline: You must communicate your choice within 3 months of departure. No retroactive changes are possible.
  • Financial advice: It is highly recommended to obtain professional financial advice before making any pension decision.
  • Tax implications: The tax implications of receiving a lump sum vs. a periodic pension can be very different depending on your country of residence.

Practical Tips

  • Check your pension estimate on the CERN HR portal regularly to plan your future.
  • Consider years of service when planning career moves. The differences between 4 and 5 years, or between 9 and 10 years, are significant.
  • Get professional financial advice before leaving CERN, especially if you have more than 5 years of service.
  • Understand the implications of transfer value versus deferred pension before making a decision.
